# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Clinic staff — doctors, nurses, receptionists, pharmacists, lab technicians, and administrators — working full shifts at desktop stations. They are power users who need speed, data density, keyboard shortcuts, and minimal friction. The interface must also be mobile-friendly for occasional tablet or phone use. Users switch between tasks rapidly: checking appointments, reviewing patient records, processing prescriptions, managing inventory, and handling billing. They work under time pressure with real patients waiting, so clarity and speed are paramount.

## Product Purpose

MCMS (Medical Center Management System) is a comprehensive clinic administration platform that manages the full patient journey — from appointment scheduling and queue management to consultations, lab orders, pharmacy prescriptions, billing, and reporting. It serves as the operational backbone of a medical center, replacing paper records and fragmented tools with a unified digital system. Success means staff can serve more patients with fewer errors, administrators have real-time visibility into operations, and the system feels invisible — fast enough that it never slows down the workflow.

## Brand Personality

Clinical, precise, trustworthy. The interface should evoke confidence and calm — staff should feel the system is reliable and professional, not playful or experimental. It should feel like a modern hospital system: authoritative but not cold, warm enough to be approachable but serious enough to be trusted with patient data. The tone is efficient and respectful of the user's time.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with gradient hero metrics and identical card grids
- Overly playful or consumer-health-app aesthetics (bright neons, rounded everything, emoji-heavy)
- Dense enterprise software that feels like a spreadsheet (SAP, old Oracle interfaces)
- Dark-mode-only tools that feel like developer dashboards
- Interfaces that prioritize decoration over data clarity

## Design Principles

1. **Data first, chrome second** — Every pixel should serve the clinical workflow. Navigation, chrome, and decoration recede; patient data, appointments, and alerts take priority.
2. **Calm under pressure** — The interface should feel stable and predictable even during peak hours. No surprise animations, no layout shifts, no visual noise that competes with critical information.
3. **Color with purpose** — Colors encode status and category, not decoration. A red dot means "urgent," a green bar means "on track." Every color must earn its place by communicating something the user needs to know.
4. **Arabic-native, not Arabic-adapted** — RTL is the primary reading direction. Typography, spacing, and layout are designed for Arabic first, with Latin as secondary. Tajawal is the primary Arabic typeface.
5. **Density without clutter** — Power users need to see a lot of information at once, but it must remain scannable. Use hierarchy, grouping, and whitespace strategically — not uniformly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as baseline
- Color must never be the sole indicator of status — pair colors with text labels, icons, or patterns
- Support for reduced motion preferences
- Sufficient color contrast in both light and dark modes, especially for the mint/teal primary palette against warm backgrounds
